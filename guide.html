CapGen closes that gap in three moves:

2 The Matching Engine

How CapGen decides what fits.

When you run Analyze Fit on an opportunity, the engine scores it against your registered profile and returns a Fit Score from 0–100 plus a recommendation. It evaluates three core factors:

⚖️ The three match factors

1. Industry-code alignment (NAICS). Is this a direct match to your primary service line, or an adjacent one? Direct matches score highest.

2. Eligibility. Do the set-aside terms (WOSB, SDVOSB, 8(a), HUBZone, small-business size standard) match your registered status? If you can't compete, you'll know immediately.

3. Capability alignment. Do your stated capabilities and past performance cover the actual scope of work — High, Medium, or Low?

Two stages, fast then deep

CapGen splits the analysis so you get the verdict instantly, then the detail:

Stage 1 — The Verdict  Appears in seconds: Fit Score, bid/no-bid recommendation, opportunity summary, eligibility review, capability match, and the rationale.

Stage 2 — The Pursuit Package  Builds behind the verdict (or on demand): performance requirements, staffing & delivery, compliance items, deadline risk, pricing considerations, a draft technical approach, a proposal checklist, and questions to ask the contracting officer.

Every analysis is grounded in the actual solicitation and your profile — never generic boilerplate. If there isn't enough information to judge a factor, CapGen says so rather than guessing.

3 Getting Started

From invite to first match in about two minutes.

  1. Open your access linkBeta testers: claim free full access at /beta, then use the personal dashboard link on the welcome screen (tap Copy Dashboard Link to save it). Subscribers sign in at /onboarding.
  2. Step 1 — Find your businessType your company name. CapGen pulls your profile from official public records — registration status, NAICS codes, and certifications. Pick your business from the list to confirm it. Not registered yet? Choose "enter NAICS codes manually."
  3. Step 2 — Tell us about your workAdd your core capabilities, past performance, team size, and set-aside certifications. These power your match accuracy and Analyze Fit scores. You can skip and complete this later — but the more you add, the sharper your results.
  4. You're setCapGen opens your pipeline — opportunities matched to your profile, refreshed daily.
💡 Tip

Spend an extra minute on the Core Capabilities field in Step 2. It's the single biggest driver of how accurately Analyze Fit reads each opportunity.

5 Reading a Fit Report

The verdict, at a glance.

Every report leads with a Fit Score and a color-coded recommendation. Read it like a traffic light:

PursueStrong fit — direct NAICS match, eligible, and your capabilities cover the scope. Prioritize it.
Pursue with CautionWorth a look, but there's a gap to close — eligibility, capability, or timeline. Read the rationale before committing resources.
WatchlistNot enough alignment to commit now, but worth keeping an eye on.
Do Not PursueClear mismatch — wrong industry code, ineligible, or capability gap too wide. Skip it and save the hours.

7 FAQ & Beta Notes

Good to know.

Where does the data come from?
Official public records — the same federal sources contracting officers post to. CapGen organizes and analyzes it; it doesn't invent opportunities.
My business isn't registered yet. Can I still use it?
Yes. In Step 1, choose "enter NAICS codes manually" and CapGen will match on those.
How often does the pipeline update?
Daily. New matched opportunities appear automatically — no manual refresh needed.
Can I save or share an analysis?
Yes. Open any Fit Report and use Export as PDF. View on SAM.gov takes you straight to the official posting.
Is the Fit Score a guarantee?
No — it's a decision aid that weighs eligibility, fit, and scope to help you prioritize. Always confirm the official solicitation before you bid.
What should beta testers focus on?
Run Analyze Fit on a handful of real opportunities and tell us whether the verdict matches your own judgment. That feedback is exactly what shapes the release.
